CSE 270: W02 Reading: Fundamentals

There are two parts of our reading assignment this week. First, you are to find three sources describing how the auto-focus works for your camera. Second, you are to visit the Association for Software Testing web site (listed at the bottom of this page).

Camera Auto-Focus

This week, we are going to test the auto-focus feature for your camera. This will be in a multi-step process:

  1. Select the camera we will test.
  2. Gather information about how the camera works.
  3. Create a test plan.
  4. Conduct the test.
  5. Report on our findings.

Throughout the course of the semester, we will learn more about each of these steps. This week, we will go through the entire process so we can see how all the steps work together.

Select a Camera.

It does not really matter which camera we use; almost all cameras have some sort of auto-focus feature. You can use the camera built into your phone, or something more advanced if you like (such as a GoPro, DSLR, or any other camera you may have). The choice is yours.

Gather Information

Find three sources that describe how the auto-focus feature works for your camera of choice. We will be summarizing these sources later in the week. Note that your sources should be higher quality than Wikipedia. Try to find sources written by acknowledged experts in the field. Note that your phone's manufacturer would be an excellent source.

For example, if you discover that your camera uses contrast detection for auto-focus, then you might need find a source or two to describes what contrast detection means and how it works.

Create a Test Plan

By the end of the week, you will create a test plan describing how you plan to verify that your camera's auto-focus works the way it is supposed to. You can learn more about this in the Lab Description.

Conduct the Test

At the end of the week, we will conduct the test plan that you are about to make. This will involve actually experimenting with your camera's auto-focus feature. Again, you can learn more about this in the Lab Description.

Report

The final part of this week's lab will be to report on your findings. In other words, does your camera's auto-focus feature work the way your research said it should?
