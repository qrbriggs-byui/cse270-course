CSE 270: W13 Overview

To maintain consistency between projects, companies need to establish policies, plans, and procedures applicable to all projects. We will learn how to assess the health and quality of a company's procedures this week.

In 1986, the U.S. Department of Defense sponsored a collection of studies to identify the common traits behind successfully managed software projects. The results of this study is called the Capability Maturity Model (CMM). The CMM was developed to objectively and systematically determine the quality of a software development team's process. This week, we will use the CMM framework to identify ways that testing activities can assist in the process of moving a project forward.

Objectives

By the end of the week, each student will be able to:

  • Arrange policies, plans, and procedures.
  • Utilize CMM Level to distinguish roles, re-consolidates, and deliverables.

Plan of the Week

This week, we will complete the following activities:

  1. Reading: Read two articles and a chapter.
  2. Create a Plan. Create a plan to move from CMM level 1 to level 5.
  3. Refine a Plan. Receive feedback from instructor. Refine the plan by addressing the shortcomings.
