CSE 270: W09 Overview

Testers have two essential roles in a software development team. First, they need to ensure that the product is built right. Second, they need to ensure that the right product is built. The former is called verification: ensuring that the product is free of defects. The latter is called validation: ensuring that the product meets the client's needs.

A big part of the verification and validation process is to ensure that defects are reported in a consistent and unambiguous manner. This is true whether the defect relates to verification or validation. In this lesson, we will learn how to create defect reports.

Objectives

By the end of the week, each student will be able to:

  • Define, compare, and contrast verification and validation.
  • Explain why bug reporting is an important part of the testing process.
  • Create high quality bug reports.

Plan of the Week

This week, we will complete the following activities:

  1. Reading: Read eight chapters.
  2. Select bugs: With your partner(s), select defects that you will use to create bug reports.
  3. Report: With your partner(s), create bug reports describing your chosen defects.
