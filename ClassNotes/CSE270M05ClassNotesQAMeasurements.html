Notes for Classroom

Overview

Besides the testing of software code, testing is valuable in producing quality products, providing maturity of the development process and determining root causes in error detection. The same principles found in software testing can be applied elsewhere in software engineering. Distinguishing between Testing and QA and how they support each other is vital in producing reliable code.  This week is a preview to QA. 

  • Students will understand the differences between QA and Testing
  • Students will understand how both disciplines help in producing reliable code.
  • Students will understand how to test quality characteristics

Objectives

By the end of the week, each student will be able to:

  • Measure Quality, Maturity, Process Improvement, Root Cause and Measurement with regards to Testing

Welcome to Week 05 Quality Assurance Measurements
This week you will be focused on the Quality Assurance portion of Testing.
Quality Assurance is a separate domain from Testing and often get confused like IT and CS. Quality Assurance focus on building the product right, or the process of building the product. Testing focuses on building the right product or make sure the product works as designed. Part of Quality Assurance is to make sure Testing is correctly done.
To test all aspects of the product, QA and Testing using a Quality Model. A Quality Model consists of various software characteristics logically arranged in grouping depending on the product’s purpose. The Model helps create the bases for the System and Regression tests and is influenced and influences the design. The Model is the pre-cursor to a test tree, meaning that groups and end node characteristics of the Model help derive and organize the tests.
As a side note, Quality Assurance is vital to having a Mature Project. According to Capability Maturity Model or CMM, Quality Assurance must have processes handle process improvement, defect prevention, benchmarks, performance collections. The processes collect metrics in both qualitative and quantitative methods to improve on the software quality characteristics.

Classroom Day 1:

  • Discussion of Quality Assurance
  • Discussion of QA Models
  • Discussion of Test Trees
  • Class Activity: Pick a product that you use everyday, that has multiple companies that produce it, pick a Quality Model, and compare three different versions of the product by measuring, and evaluating their quality. (Examples: Types of Root beer, Cars, Hamburgers, etc.)

  • Discussion of Risk Management
