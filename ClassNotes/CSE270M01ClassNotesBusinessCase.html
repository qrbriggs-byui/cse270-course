Class Notes M01: Business Case

Notes for Classroom

Overview

One of the biggest arguments that both management and software practitioners use is “there is not enough time to do testing”. Or it is the last thing on the schedule and gets time constrained.

Objectives

By the end of the week, each student will be able to:

  • Evaluate Testing Business Issues
  • Propose Business Case for Testing Overview

Before the Week Begins (Prepare)

Instructor Resources

Preparation:

  • Download the "IEEE Software Engineering Book of Knowledge". Read Chapter 4 on "Software Testing".
    • https://www.computer.org/education/bodies-of-knowledge/software-engineering
  • Find two or three articles about software testing to share with the class.

Partnership/Group work:

  • Partner Assignments: You will need to identify location of students, either by time zone or location to help with creating partnerships for the rest of the course.

Class Announcements

This week we will be covering the Fundamentals and Issues of software testing.
Many immature projects don’t have an adequate testing policy or plan. Many times development is just trying to get the product coded. A lack of testing or the schedule squeeze is one of the contributing factors of the 1970’s Software Crisis and why the majority of today’s projects. Many programmers and managers feel testing is time-consuming and don’t plan according. By the end of the week, you should feel confident that you can justify a Business Case for Software Testing.
The fundamentals of testing, presented in Software Engineering Book of Knowledge, published by IEEE, consist of Testing Levels, Techniques, Measurements, Process, and Tools.
During this course, we will be covering each of these in a broad and some area in-depth.
As part of your preparation, you need to read Chapter 4 of the SEBoK. You will use this knowledge to explore questions about testing fundamentals.
You also need to find two articles that you can write a summary on about software testing. You can use any resource, but make sure you cite the resource. You might have to read several articles, or you might know some that you have already read. I have found software testing issues in the news from several different sources, including LinkedIn, Newspapers, Facebook, and Slashdot. Â I am excited to see what source you find and the articles that you find.
The prove assignment is for you to write a business case for testing. The assignment provides a backstory and a brief overview of the assignment. The case should be 1-3 pages. I would suggest examples of solutions from your articles or your classmates as part of the risks.
The assignment’s requirements are set at 89%. To get 100%, you need to Make It Your Own. I provided some suggestions, but you can add as you see fit. You must identify what you did to Make It Your Own in the assignment comments; otherwise, I will default it at 89%.
Please make sure you have complete the assignment surveys and Ponder quizzes. The surveys will help us improve the course.

During the Week (Teach)

Class Room Day 1:

  • Module 00: Class Overview
  • Overview of Software Quality Assurance Testing

Class Room Day 2:

  • Hook Activity: Enagement: Story, Picture, Problem
    • What have you tested?
  • Overview of Software Quality Assurance Testing
    • Review Reading
      • What is testing? Faults vs Failures
        What type of errors occur in testing?
        What is the difference between QA and Testing?
        What is other roles are associated with Software Development or product?
        CM, QA, DM, PM, SE
        What Testing Levels, Techniques, measurements, and tools that you have heard about?
    • What is the purpose of Testing?
  • Overview of Ponder Assignment
    • Most Business Case resolve around either:
      Return on Investment or
      Risk/Mitigation (which lowers costs, and increase ROI)
      How would you convince your boss, manager, lead to pay more attention to testing?
      As a project manager, what would you do to ensure that testing is as important as requirements, design, implementation, and delivery?
  • Next Class: Have Articles ready

Class Room Day 3:

  • Have students share articles about Testing.
    • Ask students to give a summary of there article
    • Ask the students what are the issues? Risks? Prevention/Mitigation Suggestions, Lessons Learned
  • Overview of Ponder Assignment
    • The questions are to help the students prepare for the Prove Assignment by coming up with enough evidence to create a Business Case document and to tie back to the research that they did.

Grading/Feedback (Prove & Ponder)

Prove:

  • Student need to write a Business Case for a Test Team for HELP, Inc to develop the Dr. Mobile app for the tablet.
  • This is a pre-course evaluation of what the students know about the course. At the end of the course student will be writing a Test Plan for the Project.
  • You can provide a business case template, or let them discover their own.
  • Scenarios can and should be taken from the articles.

Ponder:

  • Student should evaluate their own points. Total points should be listed at the top of summary, and unless blandly obvious the shouldn’t, student should receive all points given.

Instructor:

  • Rubric:
    • 10 pts: Format: Heading, Readable, Grammar, Length, Citations
      • The document needs to be readable. One block of text will not do.
      • Correct Grammar and Spelling are required.
      • Length is expected to be 3-5 pages.
      • Must have a citation page
    • 20 pts: Identification of Issues/Risk
      • Must include 3-5 risks to the project about for not having testing
    • 30 pts: Potential Scenarios
      • Must include 2 scenarios that will happen if the testing team is not at fully staffed
      • Must include 2-3 options for the manager to choose.
    • 40 pts: Resolution of Issues/Risk
      • Must include 3-5 mitagation strategies that a testing team will solve.
