CSE 270: Classroom Notes M14: Testing as a Career

Notes for Classroom

Overview

Knowing the needs of the field can help in finding jobs and people for those jobs. As a professional, students need to have the ability to interpret job description posts (job recs) and customize their experience accordingly. For example, understanding the differences between a job rec that says Testing, but really is QA. Another good skill is the ability to create job descriptions that are useful for individuals to apply. (Too many times job descriptions don’t have enough information, or contains information that doesn’t match the title, or information has too many project conventions).

Objectives

By the end of the week, each student will be able to:

  • Discover professional skills and needs of companies for Testers
  • Explore job career paths

Announcement

Welcome to Week 14: Last week of the Semester

You are now a software tester. You now know how to professionally break software, or at least make sure it works, right!
For this week you are going to develop a career path. You will not be a tester your whole career. For most people being a tester is a step stone to other great career options. You are going to explore different possiblities, and gather a list of skills that you will need to further your career.
Don’t forget to take the final quiz.

Post Mortem:

After each class answer the following questions:

  • Key Analysis
    • What key components when well during the class? What didn't?
  • Class Synthesis
    • What questions did the students ask?
  • Start (New, Innovation):
    • Tips
      • If you did something differently what would it be?
    • Aha’s
      • What new ideas did you discover?
  • Stop (What didn’t work):
    • Traps
    • What would not be a good idea to do again?
  • Continue (What worked):
    • Why did the activities work well? Was it the students, the teacher (presenter), the content, or activity?
  • Humor
    • What was funny about the module? Something you said, students said, etc. that made the class more enjoyable?
