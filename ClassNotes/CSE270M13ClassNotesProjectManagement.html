CSE 270: Classroom Notes M13: Testing & Project Management

Notes for Classroom

Overview

In order to maintain consistency between projects, companies need to establish policies, plans, and procedures that are applied to all projects. Having the ability to understand the differences, read them, and produce these will be invaluable and increase ROI to the company. Understanding the different roles of CM, DM and Software Management in the role of Testing helps with the production and delivery of software.

  • Other Roles
    • Configuration Managements consists of legal accounting of 3rd party software (COTS, FOSS), project development software, integrated software as part of the delivery, and delivery constraints and scenarios.
    • Data Managements consists of document control and versioning, software development folders, tests & test data, and licensing issues.
    • Software Management consists of code repositories, versioning and patching software, delivery scheduling and peer reviews.
    • Change Management consists of how and when code, libraries and data change, and the effects of downstream changes
  • Students will distinguish between standards, regulations, policies, plans, procedures, and instructions
  • Students will explore different testing and supporting roles associated with testing
  • Students will be able to produce project products to support the health of a project.

Objectives

By the end of the week, each student will be able to:

  • Arrange policies, plans, and procedures 
  • Utilize CMM Level to distinguish roles, reconsolidates, and deliverables.
