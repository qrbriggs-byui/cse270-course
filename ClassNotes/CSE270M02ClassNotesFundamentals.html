Partnership/Group work:

During the Week (Teach)

Class Room Day 1:

  • Hook Activity: Enagement: Story, Picture, Problem
  • Content
    • What is the difference between an association, an organization and an institute?
  • Overview of Prove Assignment
    • Introduction to H.E.L.P. Inc
    • Introduction to Software Capabilities
    • Scenario: Test Training Brochure

Class Room Day 2:

  • Hook Activity: Enagement: Story, Picture, Problem
  • Lab:
    • Students will need to bring their camera to class, either laptop, cellphone, or PED.
    • Read “How does autofocus work on your smartphone”. (https://www.giffgaff.com/blog/how-does-auto-focus-work-on-your-smartphone/ ). The article discuss how different camera in today’s cell phones focus. You assignment is to determine three different ways you would test a camera’s auto focus.
    • One of the features of Dr. Mobile is its ability to gather input quickly. It does this through several sensors. One of the main sensors is the camera. The camera needs to be able to focus quickly.
    • You assignment is to determine three different ways you would test a camera’s auto focus and come up with a method to determine which type of focus algorithm and how fast.
    • Have the students write up a test and post it on the discussion board. Then have the student run each others tests.

Class Room Day 3:

  • Hook Activity: Enagement: Story, Picture, Problem
  • Research Summary of Organizations
    • About: 
      • What is their mission? Why do they exist?  
        What services do they provide? What do they publish? Training? Certifications?  
        What publications do they have? How often? Subject Matter? (i.e. ACM – Monthly, Ethic Focused) 
        What certifications do they offer and why? 
    • Community: 
      • Who do they partner with? Companies, organizations, etc 
        Why should you join? How much? What is free and what is not?  
    • Summary: 
      • Provide a brief summary of how this resource is useful for testing professional, business or project. 
    • References: 
      • What resources did you find?  Where did you find your sources? 
      • How many hours did you spend completing this task?
  • Develop a Mission Statement for your Testing Team.
    • H.E.L.P., Inc Testing Team has gotten to the size that corporate headquarters is requiring that they come up with a Mission Statement to define it purpose.
    • The Mission Statement is the driving focus of a test team. Help the student realize that the mission statement is the ownership factor.
    • This mission statement will be part of the Prove assignment and will again be used in Prove 13 Test Plan, which is the Summative assessment of the class.

Post Mortem:

After each class answer the following questions:

  • Key Analysis
    • What key components when well during the class? What didn't?
  • Class Synthesis
    • What questions did the students ask?
  • Start (New, Innovation):
    • Tips
      • If you did something differently what would it be?
    • Aha’s
      • What new ideas did you discover?
  • Stop (What didn’t work):
    • Traps
    • What would not be a good idea to do again?
  • Continue (What worked):
    • Why did the activities work well? Was it the students, the teacher (presenter), the content, or activity?
  • Humor
    • What was funny about the module? Some you said, students said, etc. that made the class more enjoyable?
