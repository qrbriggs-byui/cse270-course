Lab 08 - Executing Regression Test

Overview

Dr. Mobile is to help Emergency Medical Personal with rescue and aid in times of disaster. There is a general workflow that any medical personal does to complete their task to stabilize a person.
As you read about the workflow, imagine the following natural disasters that might happen

  • Hurricane
  • Earthquake
  • Blizzard
  • Avalanche
  • Forest Fire
  • Airplane Crash
  • Traffic Accident
  • Flooding

Workflow

Each activity in the workflows allow the EMP to accomplish some task with the Dr. Mobile Application:

  • Patient Identification
    • Collect found patient's information. Typically, this information is a one-time collection.
      • Name.
      • Fingerprints.
      • Location.
      • Race,
      • Weight/Body Mass.
      • Height.
  • Diagnose
    • o Once some quick information is collected, continue collecting finer details about the patient's condition.
      • Temperature - is the patient running a fever, infectious, or showing signs of shock.
      • Heartbeat - is the patient's heartbeat high.
      • Blood pressure - determine if the patient has high, low, or normal blood pressure.
      • Breathing - determine for substances, CO2 content.
      • Oxygen level - does the patient have low oxygen levels.
      • Skin moisture - slightly different than normal Temperature, as a patient could be in shock and sweating but not having a fever.
      • Eye exam - could tell about concussion.
    • Additional detailed scans:
      • Bone - portable X-ray could check for bone damage.
      • Neuropathy/Electrical – evaluation of nerve, brain, and respiratory systems.
      • Muscle & vain - scan for Muscle tears, bruising and internal bleeding.
      • Skin - checking for burns, veins, and radiation.
  • Treat
    • Disinfect - UV disinfection equipment, wounds.
    • Treatment method.
    • Advice from home base.
  • Monitor Patient
    • After the patient is
      • IV Monitoring.
      • External Wristband.
        • Heart.
        • O2.
        • Temperature.
        • Blood pressure.
        • Skin moisture.
    • Device to Device redundancy.
      • § Check for similar events in the same location.
      • Bi-location of scans.
  • Order Supplies
    • Collection of supplies on hand.
    • Monitory supplies in use.
    • Resource frequency checking.
    • Restocking

Assignment

You will create two sets of manual regression tests for two different situations that will test capabilities of the workflow. The following is one regression test for one of the steps of the workflow that would test the workflow of Dr. Mobile. The rest you need to feel in. Using the Dr. Mobile App simulator as reference to answer the following questions to assist you in creating the test cases. You can either use the happy path, where everything works, create a problem and point out where the system might fail.
Start with a situation, use the workflow (search, identify, diagnose, treat, monitor, order supplies) to identify the steps.
Start with this situation, Avalanche:

SITUATION – 1st Post

  • When: During Christmas break in Vail, CO, the area has been experiencing a warm and cooling spell with a significant snow amount. On December 27th, a major avalanche occurred, and a team of 3 EMP was dispatch as first responders, each equipped with Dr. Mobile.

Patient Identification

    • Who: What will be done to id the patient?
    • Assumption:
      • EMP has already logged in
      • EMP is at the DrMobileHome page, and click on the PatientMenu
    • Steps:
      • A: EMP clicks on AddPatient tab
      • R: Add Patient options is display on menu
      • A: EMP enters patient id, “Patient C”  in “Enter New Id” text field, and clicks Add Patient
      • R: Patient Name is add to the list of “Current Patients” right after Patient B.
      • R: Two additional fields “Enter First Name” and “Enter Description” appear.
      • A: EMP enters patient name “John Smith” in “Enter First Name”.
      • A: EMP enters patient description “Found in snow bank” in “Enter Description”
      • A: EMP click Done.
      • V: Fields disappear

Diagnose

    • What: What is the problem with the patient?

Treat

    • How: How will the patient be stabilized?

Monitor

    • How: How will the patient be monitored until and during transport?

Order Supplies

    • What supplies are now needed?
